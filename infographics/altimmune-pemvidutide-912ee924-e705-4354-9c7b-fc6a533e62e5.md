<section>
  <h2>Safety snapshot</h2>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-3">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Discontinuations</h3>
      <p>All adverse-event discontinuations were 6.2% for placebo and 5.1%, 19.2%, and 19.6% for the 1.2 mg, 1.8 mg, and 2.4 mg pemvidutide groups.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>GI events</h3>
      <p>Nausea was reported in 11.3% of placebo subjects and in 25.5%, 59.6%, and 51.5% of the 1.2 mg, 1.8 mg, and 2.4 mg pemvidutide groups.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Cardiac events</h3>
      <p>The source reports no AESI or MACE events and no imbalances in cardiac AEs across treatment groups.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="callout">
    <strong>Source summary</strong>
    <p>The presentation concludes that pemvidutide 2.4 mg produced robust mean weight loss at Week 48, lean loss index of 21.9%, reductions in serum lipids and blood pressure, maintained glucose homeostasis, and mostly mild to moderate gastrointestinal adverse events common to incretin-based agents.</p>
  </div>
