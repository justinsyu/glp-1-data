<section>
  <h2>Why glucagon content matters</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="GLP-1 glucagon dual receptor agonist rationale">
    <div>GLP-1 effects: appetite and inflammation</div>
    <div>Glucagon effects: liver, energy expenditure, adipose browning, lipolysis</div>
    <div>Target outcomes: body weight and liver fat</div>
    <div>MASH objective: inflammation and resulting fibrosis</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Non-incretin MASH agents</h3>
      <p>The source table reports limited body-weight changes for advanced non-incretin MASH agents, including -2% for obeticholic acid, no change for resmetirom, +3.1% for lanifibranor, -0.6% for pegozafermin, and -2.6% for efruxifermin.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>GLP-1 and GIP liver fat effects</h3>
      <p>The source describes GLP-1 and GIP agents as having modest liver fat effects, listing -34% for semaglutide at Week 24, -57% for semaglutide at Week 48, and -57% for tirzepatide at Week 52.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>
