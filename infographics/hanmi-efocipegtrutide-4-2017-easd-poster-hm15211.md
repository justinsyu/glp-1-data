<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Study design">
    <div>9-week-old C57Bl/6 male mice entered the MPTP Parkinson's disease model.</div>
    <div>MPTP was administered once daily for 7 days to affect the nigrostriatal system.</div>
    <div>HM15211 was given once at 2.5 or 5.03 nmol/kg after the first MPTP administration.</div>
    <div>Motor function was evaluated by traction, pole, and rotarod tests before sacrifice.</div>
    <div>Brain sections and striatum lysates were analyzed for histology, cytokines, and oxidative stress.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Source Aim</h3>
      <p>The poster investigated HM15211 therapeutic potential in an MPTP-induced Parkinson's disease mouse model across behavior, pathology, and molecular endpoints.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Analysis Frame</h3>
      <p>The source reports one-way ANOVA followed by Dunnett post-hoc analysis, with p below 0.05 considered statistically significant.</p>
    </article>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211 significantly improved MPTP-induced motor impairments in three behavior tests in a dose-dependent manner.</li>
    <li>Tyrosine hydroxylase-positive neurons in substantia nigra and staining density in striatum were reduced by MPTP and protected by HM15211.</li>
    <li>HM15211 changed inflammatory cytokine expression and reduced a lipid peroxidation byproduct in the MPTP Parkinson's disease model.</li>
    <li>The source concludes that even after a single HM15211 injection, neuroprotective effects were shown against 7 days of repeated MPTP injection.</li>
  </ul>
