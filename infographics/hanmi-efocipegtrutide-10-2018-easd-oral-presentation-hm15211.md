<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Oral presentation model design">
    <div>HM15211 is described as a GLP-1/GIP/glucagon triple agonist conjugated with human IgG Fc through a flexible linker.</div>
    <div>The objective was to assess efficacy and related modes of action in Parkinson's disease and diabetic Alzheimer-like disease mouse models.</div>
    <div>Subchronic Parkinson's model: MPTP was dosed daily, with sacrifice at day 7.</div>
    <div>Chronic Parkinson's model: MPTP plus probenecid was dosed twice weekly, with HM15211 once weekly and sacrifice at 6 weeks.</div>
    <div>Diabetic Alzheimer-like pathology model: db/db male mice received HM15211 1.08 nmol/kg Q2D for 12 weeks.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Mechanistic Rationale</h3>
      <p>The source links incretin hormones in the central nervous system with neurite outgrowth, progenitor proliferation, reduced inflammation, and reduced glutamate neurotoxicity.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Development Context</h3>
      <p>The HM15211 profile slide states that a first-in-human clinical study in healthy obese subjects had recently been completed.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>In the MPTP/probenecid chronic Parkinson's disease model, HM15211 inhibited alpha-synuclein increase, a pathological biomarker highlighted by the source.</li>
    <li>In aged db/db mice, Alzheimer-like A beta 1-42 and AGE accumulation were shown and reversed by HM15211 treatment.</li>
    <li>The presentation attributes neuroprotective effects to anti-inflammatory effects through altered cytokine expression and reduced lipid peroxidation.</li>
    <li>The source concludes that HM15211 might have therapeutic potential for neurodegenerative diseases.</li>
  </ul>
