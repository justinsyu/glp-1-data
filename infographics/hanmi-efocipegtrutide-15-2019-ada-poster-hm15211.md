<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Neurodegenerative disease model design">
    <div>Chronic Parkinson's disease model: MPTP plus probenecid was injected twice a week for 5 weeks.</div>
    <div>HM15211 was administered once weekly for 6 weeks in the chronic Parkinson's disease model.</div>
    <div>Diabetic Alzheimer-like pathology: db/db mice received HM15211 every 2 days for 12 weeks.</div>
    <div>Multiple sclerosis model: relapsing-remitting EAE was induced in SJL mice.</div>
    <div>EAE mice were treated with HM15211 from day 1 to evaluate prophylactic effects.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Peripheral To CNS Rationale</h3>

    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Source Scope</h3>
      <p>The poster tested motor behavior, neuronal staining, inflammatory cytokines, oxidative stress markers, amyloid beta 1-42, and EAE histopathology endpoints.</p>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211 inhibited increased alpha-synuclein in the MPTP/probenecid chronic Parkinson's disease model and restored motor function.</li>
    <li>HM15211 reversed Alzheimer-like pathological features including A beta 1-42 and AGE accumulation in aged db/db mice.</li>
    <li>HM15211 reduced EAE clinical score and demyelination in the relapsing-remitting EAE model of multiple sclerosis.</li>
    <li>The poster concludes that HM15211 might have therapeutic potential for neurodegenerative diseases through anti-inflammatory properties.</li>
  </ul>
