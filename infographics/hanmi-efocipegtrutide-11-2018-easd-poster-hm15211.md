<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Study design">
    <div>MC3T3-E1 mouse osteoblast cells were treated with HM15211 for mechanism studies.</div>
    <div>DIO osteoporosis rats were induced by OVX and 60% kcal fat diet in 5-week-old female SD rats.</div>
    <div>Groups received vehicle, liraglutide 25 nmol/kg BID, or HM15211 2.2 or 4.4 nmol/kg Q2D.</div>
    <div>Serum Glu-OC, OPG, and PINP were measured by ELISA.</div>
    <div>A food-restricted group was included to match body weight loss with HM15211 2.2 nmol/kg.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Poster Aim</h3>
      <p>The source states that the study investigated whether HM15211 prevents bone loss under severe weight loss and explored the underlying mechanism of action.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Weight-Matched Question</h3>
      <p>The EASD source adds a food-restricted comparator to ask whether bone findings were explained by weight loss alone.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211 lowered Glu-OC and increased OPG and PINP compared with vehicle and liraglutide treated groups in the obese-osteoporosis rat model.</li>
    <li>HM15211 showed comparable femur BMD to vehicle while producing greater weight loss than liraglutide.</li>
    <li>During matched weight loss, HM15211 preserved femur BMD and kept Glu-OC lower than food restriction.</li>
    <li>The source concludes that HM15211 might provide potent weight loss without bone loss.</li>
  </ul>
