<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Study design">
    <div>Chronic PD model: MPTP plus probenecid injection twice weekly for 5 weeks, with HM15211 once weekly for 6 weeks.</div>
    <div>PD behavioral tests included traction, pole test, and rotarod; tissue readouts included TH+ cells, alpha-synuclein, Iba1, IFN-gamma, and IL-10.</div>
    <div>AD progression model: 6-week-old db/db mice received subcutaneous HM15211 once every 2 days for 12 weeks.</div>
    <div>AD readouts included A beta 1-42, AGE, IL-1 beta, IFN-gamma, and activated microglia in cortex and hippocampus.</div>
    <div>The poster frames obesity, type 2 diabetes, and NASH as risk factors for progressive neurodegenerative diseases such as PD and AD.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Prior Context</h3>
      <p>The abstract states that HM15211 had previously shown neuroprotective effects in an MPTP-induced subacute PD mouse model.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Development Context</h3>
      <p>The source describes HM15211 as a long-acting GLP-1/GIP/glucagon triple agonist being developed for obesity and NASH.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>In the MPTP/probenecid chronic PD model, HM15211 inhibited the increase of alpha-synuclein, a pathological biomarker of PD.</li>
    <li>In aged db/db mice, AD-related pathological characters such as A beta 1-42 and AGE accumulation were reversed by HM15211 treatment.</li>
    <li>The source links HM15211's neuroprotective effects to altered cytokine expression and anti-inflammatory effects.</li>
    <li>The poster concludes that HM15211 might have therapeutic potential for neurodegenerative diseases.</li>
  </ul>
