<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="BDL study design">
    <div>C57BL/6 mice underwent BDL, or bile-duct ligation, to induce liver fibrosis.</div>
    <div>Study 1: drug treatment for 2 weeks starting at week 0.</div>
    <div>Study 2: drug treatment for 2 weeks starting at week 2.</div>
    <div>BDL mice were used to evaluate direct anti-fibrotic effect and mechanism of action.</div>
    <div>Comparator in several readouts: obeticholic acid 30 mg/kg once daily.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Proposed Mechanism</h3>
      <p>The source describes HM15211 as inhibiting hepatic stellate cell activation and activated HSC fibrogenesis in a proposed anti-fibrotic mechanism.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Clinical Context</h3>
      <p>The conclusion states that clinical studies in biopsy-proven NASH and fibrosis patients were ongoing at the time of the poster.</p>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211, a long-acting GLP-1/GIP/glucagon triple agonist, is designed to treat NASH and fibrosis.</li>
    <li>In BDL mice, HM15211 improved fibrosis regardless of model induction period.</li>
    <li>The poster concludes that better efficacy than OCA highlights the anti-fibrotic effect of HM15211.</li>
    <li>HM15211 reduced TGF-beta production and inhibited collagen secretion by HSC in the presence of TGF-beta.</li>
  </ul>
