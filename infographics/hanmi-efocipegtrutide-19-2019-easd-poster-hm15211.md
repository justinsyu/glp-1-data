<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Study design">
    <div>MCD-diet mice were used to evaluate steatosis, inflammation, liver function, NASH, and fibrosis.</div>
    <div>HM15211 treatment lasted 4 to 5 weeks after 6 to 12 weeks of MCD-diet induction.</div>
    <div>Mouse readouts included hepatic TG, TBARS, ALT, bilirubin, marker gene expression, NAS, hydroxyproline, and fibrosis score.</div>
    <div>Obese NASH and fibrosis monkeys had BMI greater than 40 kg/m2 and NAS plus fibrosis score greater than 7.</div>
    <div>Monkey treatment lasted 12 weeks, including a 3-week titration period, with DEXA and liver biopsy analyses.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Multiple Pathway Rationale</h3>
      <p>The poster states that NASH and fibrosis involve multiple biological pathways, and that HM15211 was developed as a long-acting GLP-1/GIP/glucagon triple agonist to target the disease process.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Translational Frame</h3>
      <p>The source uses both rodent disease models and biopsy-proven obese NASH and fibrosis monkeys to evaluate whether mouse findings translated to NHP.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211 was designed to treat NASH and fibrosis by aiming at multiple pathways involved in disease progression.</li>
    <li>In rodent NASH models, HM15211 reduced liver fat and inflammation marker expression, leading to NASH resolution.</li>
    <li>HM15211 showed potential to improve fibrosis in rodent NASH and fibrosis models regardless of fibrosis stage.</li>
    <li>The poster states that beneficial NASH and fibrosis effects were translated in obese NASH NHP.</li>
