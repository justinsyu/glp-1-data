<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Study design">
    <div>DIO mice were used to test hepatic lipid metabolism gene expression after 4 weeks of HM15211.</div>
    <div>AMLN-diet mice were used to measure NAFLD activity score and hepatic TG after 4 weeks of treatment.</div>
    <div>MCD-diet mice were induced for 6 weeks, then treated for 4 weeks to evaluate NASH and fibrosis.</div>
    <div>MCD readouts included hepatic TG, TBARS, ALT, bilirubin, marker gene expression, MRI, NAS, Sirius red staining, and hydroxyproline.</div>
    <div>Liraglutide 50 nmol/kg BID served as a comparator in multiple source tables.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Mechanistic Frame</h3>
      <p>The source presents HM15211 as a long-acting GLP-1/GIP/glucagon triple agonist with potential NASH effects through liver lipid metabolism, oxidative stress, inflammation, liver function, and fibrosis.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Liver Targeting Signal</h3>
      <p>The poster states that HM15211 was preferentially distributed to liver, the main target organ for glucagon action.</p>
    </article>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211 improved hepatic lipid metabolism related gene expression in DIO mice and reduced NASH prognosis markers in AMLN-diet mice.</li>
    <li>In MCD-diet mice, HM15211 reduced hepatic lipid contents, oxidative stress, blood ALT, and bilirubin.</li>
    <li>HM15211 reduced genes linked to hepatic inflammation, HSC activation, and fibrosis.</li>
    <li>The poster concludes that HM15211 may offer therapeutic potential for NASH and fibrosis as well as obesity.</li>
  </ul>
