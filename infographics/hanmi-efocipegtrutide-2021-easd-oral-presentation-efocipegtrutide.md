<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="2021 EASD NASH study map">
    <div>HM15211 is presented as a single drug moiety with glucagon, GIP, and GLP-1 activity.</div>
    <div>The source states that extended half-life allows once weekly dosing and that the agonist was optimized for liver targeting.</div>
    <div>Tissue distribution and hepatic lipid metabolism were compared with liraglutide in mice.</div>
    <div>AMLN Study 1 used AMLN diet for 49 weeks and drug treatment for 12 weeks.</div>
    <div>AMLN Study 2 used AMLN diet for 37 weeks and drug treatment for 12 weeks, with GLP-1, GLP/GIP, and GLP/GCG comparators.</div>
    <div>Mechanistic THP-1 assays tested monocyte migration, adhesion, and pro-inflammatory cytokine expression and secretion.</div>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211 is described as a glucagon/GIP/GLP-1 triple agonist with activity features designed to treat NASH and fibrosis by targeting multiple aspects of disease.</li>
    <li>The presentation says previous studies showed greater liver fat reduction than GLP-1RA through hepatic lipid metabolism reprogramming after liver preferential distribution.</li>
    <li>In AMLN-diet mice, HM15211 reduced steatosis, inflammation, ballooning, and composite NAS in the source tables.</li>
    <li>The source states that HM15211 might provide improved efficacy for NASH, and that a phase 2b study was ongoing in biopsy-proven NASH subjects in the US.</li>
  </ul>
