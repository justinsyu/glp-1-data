<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Single ascending dose design">
    <div>Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled First-In-Human trial.</div>
    <div>Obese but otherwise healthy adult subjects, mean age 45.7 years.</div>
    <div>Mean BMI 33.6 kg/m<sup>2</sup>; 51.2% male.</div>
    <div>Five sequential HM15211 dose cohorts: 0.01, 0.02, 0.04, 0.08, and 0.12 mg/kg.</div>
    <div>Primary objectives: safety, tolerability, and PK after single SC doses.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Mechanistic Rationale</h3>
      <p>The source describes HM15211 as a GLP-1/GIP/glucagon triple-receptor co-agonist conjugated with a human IgG Fc fragment to extend half-life.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Development Context</h3>
      <p>The poster states that single doses showed PK profiles suitable for weekly administration and that studies in obese NAFLD/NASH patients would follow.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211 was safe and well tolerated after a single SC injection in healthy subjects.</li>
    <li>PK profiles up to 0.12 mg/kg showed dose-dependent circulating HM15211 exposure and potential for weekly dosing.</li>
    <li>Reduction in incretins and glucagon confirmed the hypothesized effects of HM15211 on those biomarkers.</li>
    <li>A Phase 1, 12-week multiple ascending dose study was ongoing in obese NAFLD subjects at the time of the poster.</li>
  </ul>
