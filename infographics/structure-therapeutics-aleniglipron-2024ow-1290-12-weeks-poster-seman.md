<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="GSBR-1290 study design">
    <div>Two studies in participants living with overweight or obesity</div>
    <div>Phase 2a study: N = 64, randomized 3:1 to GSBR-1290 or placebo</div>
    <div>Phase 2a titration: 5 mg starting dose up to 120 mg once daily</div>
    <div>Capsule-to-tablet PK study: N = 54 randomized in three cohorts</div>
    <div>PK study titration: one-, two-, three-, or four-week schemes up to 60 mg or 120 mg</div>
  </div>
  <div class="callout">
    <strong>Combined efficacy analysis</strong>
    <p>The source states that percent change in body weight from baseline was analyzed across obesity cohorts with a mixed model for repeated measures. The 120 mg arms from both studies were combined, and placebo groups from both studies were combined.</p>
  </div>

<section>
  <h2>Safety And PK Context</h2>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Liver profile</h3>
      <p>The source reports no DILI, no permanent liver enzyme elevations, and no study discontinuations related to liver function. ALT or AST at 5 times ULN or higher was 0 across reported groups.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Once-daily rationale</h3>
      <p>The poster states that GSBR-1290 was designed for approximately 24-hour drug exposure to enable once-daily dosing, and that PK findings support once-daily dosing with generally proportional exposure between 60 mg and 120 mg.</p>
    </article>
