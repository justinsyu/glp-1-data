<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Animal model strategy">
    <div>HM15211 is described as a long-acting GLP-1/GIP/glucagon triple agonist with liver preferential distribution.</div>
    <div>AMLN-diet mice were induced for 28 weeks and treated for 4 weeks, with body weight, hepatic TG, and steatosis score analyzed.</div>
    <div>MCD-diet mice were induced for 6 to 12 weeks and treated for 4 to 5 weeks, depending on the study.</div>
    <div>MCD readouts included hepatic TG, TBARS, ALT, total bilirubin, marker expression, NAS, hydroxyproline, fibrosis score, and Sirius red staining.</div>
    <div>The source conclusion frames HM15211 effects as acting on lipotoxicity and inflammation in NASH and fibrosis models.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Triple Agonist Profile</h3>
      <p>The presentation states that HM15211 is conjugated with a human IgG Fc fragment through a flexible linker and had recently completed a first-in-human study in healthy obese subjects.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Disease Coverage</h3>
      <p>The source compares AMLN-diet obesity-associated disease with MCD-diet lean NASH with mild to advanced fibrosis.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211 directly reduced liver fat and possibly inflammation, which the source describes as suggesting therapeutic potential in NASH and fibrosis.</li>
    <li>In AMLN-diet mice, HM15211 provided efficient weight loss and completely reversed steatosis, while ASK1 and FXR comparators did not.</li>
    <li>In MCD-diet mice, HM15211 reduced liver fat, oxidative stress, marker expression including HSC activation, and NAFLD activity score.</li>
    <li>The presentation states that a clinical study in NASH patients was planned for human efficacy translation.</li>
  </ul>
