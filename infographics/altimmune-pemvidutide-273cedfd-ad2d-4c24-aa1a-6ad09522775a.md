<section>
  <h2>Cardioinflammatory lipid signals</h2>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Small, dense LDL particle number</h3>
      <p>Median small, dense LDL particle number moved from 785 to 760 in placebo, and Week 12 medians were 700, 640, and 635 in the 1.2 mg, 1.8 mg, and 2.4 mg pemvidutide groups.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Sphingolipids</h3>
      <p>For all 49 sphingolipid subspecies, percent changes were -2.0% with placebo and -23%, -30%, and -27% with pemvidutide 1.2 mg, 1.8 mg, and 2.4 mg.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Lysophospholipids</h3>
      <p>Percent changes were -10% with placebo and -13%, -25%, and -25% with pemvidutide 1.2 mg, 1.8 mg, and 2.4 mg.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Stable statin subgroup</h3>
      <p>The source states that MOMENTUM participants on stable statin therapy had further decreases in serum lipids.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

    <p>The presentation concludes that pemvidutide treatment led to substantial and clinically meaningful reductions in triglycerides, total cholesterol, and LDL, with significant reductions in small dense LDL, sphingolipids, and lysophospholipids.</p>
