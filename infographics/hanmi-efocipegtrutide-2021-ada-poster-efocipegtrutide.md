<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Preclinical fibrosis study schema">
    <div>Study 1: AMLN/TAA mice were used to evaluate hepatic fibrosis.</div>
    <div>Study 1: HM15211 was administered during the last 8 weeks of a 16 week model.</div>
    <div>Study 2: CD-HFD mice were used for efficacy comparison across incretin treatments.</div>
    <div>In vitro work tested TGF-beta-induced HSC activation in LX-2 cells and rat primary HSCs.</div>
    <div>THP-1 macrophages were used to evaluate IL-4/IL-13-induced M2 polarization.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Therapeutic Rationale</h3>
      <p>The abstract describes fibrosis progression as involving complex biological pathways and frames HM15211 as a multidisciplinary therapeutic approach.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Mode Of Action</h3>
      <p>The source describes proposed direct anti-fibrotic effects through inhibition of M2 polarization, HSC activation, and activated HSC fibrogenesis.</p>
    </article>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211 is designed to treat NASH and fibrosis by targeting multiple aspects of the disease.</li>
    <li>In AMLN/TAA and CD-HFD mice, HM15211 showed robust anti-fibrotic effects.</li>
    <li>The source says treatment benefits were more pronounced than acylated GLP-1RA, GLP/GIP, or GLP/GIP dual agonist comparators.</li>
    <li>HM15211 inhibited collagen secretion by TGF-beta in HSC, which the source says explains direct anti-fibrotic effects.</li>
    <li>A phase 2b study in biopsy-proven NASH subjects was ongoing in the United States at the time of the poster.</li>
  </ul>
