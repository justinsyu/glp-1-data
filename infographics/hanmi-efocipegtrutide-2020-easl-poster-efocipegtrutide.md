<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Phase 1b/2a study design">
    <div>Non-diabetic obese subjects with NAFLD and MRI-PDFF of at least 10% were enrolled.</div>
    <div>The trial was multicenter, randomized, placebo-controlled, and multiple ascending dose.</div>
    <div>Subjects were randomized to HM15211 or placebo in a 3:1 ratio, with 12 subjects per cohort planned.</div>
    <div>Primary objectives covered safety, tolerability, pharmacokinetics, and liver fat reduction.</div>
    <div>Exploratory objectives assessed additional pharmacodynamic properties after multiple doses.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Baseline Population</h3>
      <p>The abstract reports 50% women, mean age 46 years, mean BMI 36 kg/m2, and mean baseline liver fat by MRI-PDFF of 19.2%.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Therapeutic Rationale</h3>
      <p>The source describes HM15211 as a GLP-1/GIP/GCG triple agonist with liver-targeted distribution, weekly dosing potential, and modes of action across steatosis, inflammation, and fibrosis.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211 significantly decreased liver fat content and body weight at 8 and 12 weeks.</li>
    <li>The poster reports dose-dependent liver fat reduction with escalating HM15211 doses.</li>
    <li>At 0.08 mg/kg, week 12 mean liver fat change was -81.2%, and all evaluable subjects met both >=30% and >=50% liver fat reduction thresholds.</li>
    <li>Further development of HM15211 as a treatment for NASH was warranted, and a phase 2b trial was ongoing at the time of the poster.</li>
  </ul>
