<section>
  <h2>Study at a glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="MASLD NIT study design">
    <div>64 subjects with MASLD</div>
    <div>Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial</div>
    <div>Weekly subcutaneous pemvidutide or placebo</div>
    <div>24-week outcome measures</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Key eligibility</h3>
      <p>BMI &ge;28 kg/m2 and LFC by MRI-PDFF &ge;10%, FibroScan LSM &lt;10kPa, HbA1c &lt;9.5%, and ALT and AST &le;75 IU/L.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Outcome measures</h3>
      <p>FAST score &lt;0.35 at week 24, and simultaneous MRI-PDFF &ge;30% and ALT &ge;17 IU/L reduction at week 24 among subjects with ALT &ge;30 IU/L at baseline.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Source conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Pemvidutide treatment resulted in reductions of up to 76.4% in relative LFC, and 15.2 IU/L in serum ALT at 24 weeks.</li>
    <li>Reductions in LFC correlated with improvements in NITs.</li>
    <li>In a subset of subjects with intermediate-to-high risk of MASH, pemvidutide treatment was associated with improvement in the FAST score.</li>
    <li>In a subset of subjects with ALT &ge;30 IU/L at baseline, pemvidutide treatment resulted in a significantly greater combined ALT/MRI-PDFF response rate compared to placebo, indicating greater likelihood of histologic response.</li>
    <li>Pemvidutide is being evaluated in an ongoing 24-week Phase 2b biopsy-driven MASH trial (IMPACT: NCT05989711).</li>
  </ul>
</section>
