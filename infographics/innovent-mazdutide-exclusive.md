<section>
  <h2>Diabesity Fit</h2>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Target population</h3>
      <p>The deck positions OXM3 against overlapping diabetes and obesity needs in China, citing 12% adult obesity, 12% adult diabetes, more than 30% diabetes among overweight or obese people, and more than 40% overweight or obesity among people with diabetes.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Mechanism</h3>
      <p>OXM3 is described as an engineered analog of oxyntomodulin, a naturally occurring 37-amino acid peptide hormone, and as a dual agonist targeting both GLP-1 receptor and glucagon receptor.</p>
    </article>
  </div>

    <thead><tr><th>Source claim</th><th>How the presentation uses it</th></tr></thead>

  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">

    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>After the license</h3>

  </div>
  <div class="callout">
    <strong>Source takeaway</strong>
    <p>Innovent describes the OXM3 partnership as a transformative opportunity to move from primarily an oncology company toward an oncology and metabolic disease company, with diabetes a key focus area going forward.</p>
