<section>
  <h2>Study Design</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Phase 1 study design">
    <div>SAD, healthy participants, two sequential cohorts, eight participants each, randomized 6:2.</div>
    <div>Cohort A1: 1 mg, 4 mg, 10 mg, 25 mg.</div>
    <div>Cohort A2: 50 mg, 100 mg, 200 mg, 300 mg.</div>
    <div>MAD, T2D participants, four sequential cohorts, 12 participants each, randomized 9:3.</div>
    <div>5 mg, 10 mg, 30 mg, or 50 mg once daily for up to 28 days.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>SAD Population</h3>
      <p>Healthy adult participants aged 18 to 65 years, with BMI between 18.0 and 32.0 kg/m<sup>2</sup>. In the SAD study, 39 participants were screened, 19 participants randomized including four replacements, and 16 participants completed the study.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>MAD Population</h3>
      <p>Adult participants aged 18 to 70 years, with BMI between 24.0 and 40.0 kg/m<sup>2</sup>, HbA1c between 7.0 and 10.5%, and T2D treated with metformin. The MAD study screened 188 participants, randomized 52, and 48 (92.3%) completed the study.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Safety</h2>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>SAD Safety</h3>
      <p>There were no serious adverse events, deaths or discontinuations due to treatment-emergent adverse events. Dose-dependent increases in adverse events were observed, and nausea and vomiting were the most common adverse events observed at doses greater than or equal to 50 mg.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>MAD Safety</h3>
      <p>There were no serious adverse events or deaths. The source reports two discontinuations due to treatment-emergent adverse events: QTc prolongation on Day 1 (Cohort 3) and asymptomatic, transient liver enzyme elevation on Day 7 (Cohort 4), both of which resolved with no intervention.</p>
