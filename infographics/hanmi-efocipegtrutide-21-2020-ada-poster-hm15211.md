<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="AMLN/TAA study design">
    <div>C57BL/6 mice were fed an AMLN diet containing 40% fat, 22% fructose, and 2% cholesterol from week 0 to 16.</div>
    <div>TAA was given at 50, 100, 200, and 300 mg/kg, then maintained at 400 mg/kg three times weekly from week 8 to 16.</div>
    <div>Drug treatment occurred from week 9 to 16 in the experimental scheme.</div>
    <div>The poster frames the AMLN/TAA model as evaluating anti-inflammatory mechanism and anti-fibrotic effect.</div>
    <div>THP-1 macrophages were used to test LPS-induced pro-inflammatory cytokine secretion.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Proposed Mode Of Action</h3>

    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Clinical Translation</h3>
      <p>The conclusion states that clinical studies in biopsy-proven NASH and fibrosis patients were ongoing at the time of the poster.</p>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211 is designed to treat NASH and fibrosis by targeting multiple aspects of disease biology.</li>
    <li>In AMLN/TAA mice, HM15211 improved inflammation according to the source conclusion.</li>
    <li>Mechanistically, the poster says HM15211 inhibited pro-inflammatory cytokine secretion in THP-1 macrophage.</li>
    <li>The poster concludes that HM15211 reversed hepatic collagen deposition in AMLN/TAA mice, demonstrating fibrosis improvement in addition to anti-inflammatory effect.</li>
  </ul>
