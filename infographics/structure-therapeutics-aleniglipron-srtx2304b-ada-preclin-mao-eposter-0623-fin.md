<section>
  <h2>Small Molecule Rationale</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Poster rationale">
    <div>Peptidic GLP-1 receptor agonists are established treatments for T2D and obesity.</div>
    <div>The source identifies injection requirements and cold chain storage as limits for many peptide GLP-1RAs.</div>
    <div>GSBR-1290 is described as a highly potent, orally available, fully biased small molecule GLP-1R agonist.</div>
    <div>The poster evaluates in vitro signaling, insulin secretion in a human pancreatic beta cell line, and efficacy in nonhuman primates.</div>
  </div>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>GSBR-1290 is described as a highly potent, orally available, fully biased small molecule GLP-1 receptor agonist.</li>
    <li>The source reports glucose dependent insulin secretion in EndoC-bH5 cells, indicating target engagement in physiologically relevant insulin secreting cells.</li>
    <li>In nonhuman primates, GSBR-1290 stimulated insulin secretion, improved glucose tolerance, and reduced food intake and body weight.</li>
    <li>The poster states that the Phase 1 SAD study with GSBR-1290 had been completed.</li>
  </ul>
