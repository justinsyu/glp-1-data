<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="2019 ADA dyslipidemia poster study map">
    <div>High-fat and high-fructose-fed hamsters were administered HM15211, evolocumab, rosuvastatin, or vehicle, and lipid profiles were monitored.</div>
    <div>Normal mice underwent oral lipid tolerance testing with olive oil followed by blood TG monitoring.</div>
    <div>HepG2 cells were used for western blot, LDL uptake, HMGCR activity, and lipid metabolism gene expression studies.</div>
    <div>Hamster liver tissue was prepared at study end to evaluate LDLR and HMGCR protein expression.</div>
    <div>The source frames HM15211 as a multi-pathway approach to dyslipidemia, unlike drugs with single primary targets.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Background Need</h3>
      <p>The abstract states that dyslipidemia is a well-established cardiovascular disease risk factor and that heterogeneity may limit single-target approaches.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Source Thesis</h3>
      <p>The poster proposes that GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon triple agonism could provide broader lipid lowering efficacy by affecting multiple lipid metabolism pathways.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Proposed Multi-Step Effect</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Proposed modes of action for lipid lowering">
    <div>Lipid tolerance increase leading to lipid absorption decrease.</div>
    <div>LDLR increase leading to LDL clearance increase.</div>
    <div>HMGCR decrease leading to cholesterol synthesis decrease.</div>
    <div>FFA synthesis gene decrease plus beta-oxidation gene increase leading to FFA and TG degradation increase.</div>
    <div>ApoA1 and ABCA1 increase leading to HDL synthesis increase.</div>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>In dyslipidemia hamsters, HM15211 provided greater cholesterol lowering than commercial dyslipidemia drugs such as evolocumab and rosuvastatin.</li>
    <li>The source identifies inhibition of lipid absorption, enhanced LDL clearance, inhibition of cholesterol synthesis, improved FFA metabolism, and potential HDL synthesis as responsible modes of action.</li>
    <li>The poster concludes that HM15211 might be a good therapeutic option for dyslipidemia patients.</li>
  </ul>
