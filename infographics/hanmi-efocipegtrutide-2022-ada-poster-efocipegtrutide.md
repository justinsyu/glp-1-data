<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="TAA study design">
    <div>TAA, or thioacetamide, was injected for 12 weeks with gradually increased dosing.</div>
    <div>HM15211 was administered subcutaneously during the last 10 weeks.</div>
    <div>Liver tissues were evaluated with H&amp;E, Sirius red staining, hydroxyproline assay, and qPCR.</div>
    <div>Blood pro-inflammatory cytokines were measured by multiplex assay.</div>
    <div>Mechanistic studies used THP-1 human monocyte cells and LX2 human hepatic stellate cells.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Therapeutic Rationale</h3>
      <p>The source describes HM15211 as a long-acting glucagon/GIP/GLP-1 triple agonist conjugated to a human IgG Fc fragment through a short PEG linker.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Pre-existing Fibrosis Context</h3>
      <p>The poster notes that baseline fibrosis score at week 2 was 1.0, while the HM15211 score after treatment was 0.7.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>
