<section>
  <h2>Trial design</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="MOMENTUM obesity trial design">
    <div>Phase 2, 48-week trial</div>
    <div>Randomized 1:1:1:1 by gender and baseline BMI</div>
    <div>Placebo, 1.2 mg, 1.8 mg, or 2.4 mg weekly</div>
    <div>MRIs quantified lean mass, VAT, SAT, and TAT</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>MRI subset</h3>
      <p>Body composition MRIs were completed in 67 subjects, including 50 receiving pemvidutide, at baseline and Week 48.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Clinical frame</h3>
      <p>The source states that preservation of lean mass may reduce the risk of falls and fractures, development of co-morbidities, and higher rates of all-cause mortality.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Source conclusions</h2>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Lean mass</h3>
      <p>The source concludes that a Lean Loss Ratio of 21.9% represents preservation of lean mass, and that preservation was maintained in older subjects.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Adipose tissue</h3>
      <p>The source concludes that pemvidutide produced preferential reduction of visceral adipose tissue, which may be associated with reduced cardiovascular disease risk.</p>
    </article>
