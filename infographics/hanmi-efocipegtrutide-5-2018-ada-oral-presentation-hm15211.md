<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Combination study schema">
    <div>HM15211 is described as a long-acting GLP-1/GIP/glucagon triple agonist conjugated with human IgG Fc through a flexible linker.</div>
    <div>The presentation tested HM15211 as a partner for HM12460A, Hanmi's once-weekly basal insulin candidate.</div>
    <div>Genetic diabetes model: db/db mice received 2 weeks of treatment, with non-fasting blood glucose, HbA1c, and body weight endpoints.</div>
    <div>Acquired diabetes model: DIO/STZ rats received 8 weeks of treatment, with blood glucose, HbA1c, and body weight endpoints.</div>
    <div>The source also profiles HM15211 as promoting weight loss through white adipose tissue browning and increased energy expenditure.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Combination Rationale</h3>
      <p>The source hypothesizes that pairing basal insulin with HM15211 could provide benefits in glycemic control and body weight management.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Long-Acting Context</h3>
      <p>The HM15211 profile slide reports half-life ranges of 42.7 to 55 hours in mice and 82.8 to 85.7 hours in rats.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>The source frames HM15211 as an additional combination partner for basal insulin beyond prandial insulin and GLP-1RA approaches.</li>
    <li>In db/db mice, HM12460A plus HM15211 lowered HbA1c to 5.7% and produced -19% body weight change after 2 weeks.</li>
    <li>In DIO/STZ rats, the combination lowered AUCBG to 8000 day*mg/dL and HbA1c to 3.7% after 8 weeks.</li>
    <li>The presentation conclusion emphasizes improved glycemic control and particularly effective body weight loss with the HM15211 combination.</li>
  </ul>
