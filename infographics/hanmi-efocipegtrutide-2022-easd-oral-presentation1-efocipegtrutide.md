<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="TAA study design">
    <div>The source describes HM15211 as a glucagon/GIP/GLP-1 triple agonist designed to treat NASH and fibrosis.</div>
    <div>TAA induction was conducted for 12 weeks in the mouse liver injury and fibrosis model.</div>
    <div>HM15211 treatment ran from week 2 to week 12 in the study design.</div>
    <div>The source used histology, hepatic hydroxyproline, hepatic marker genes, and blood liver enzymes as readouts.</div>
    <div>In vitro mechanistic assays evaluated macrophage activation, cytokine secretion, HSC activation, and collagen secretion.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Triple Agonist Profile</h3>
      <p>The presentation describes HM15211 as a single drug moiety with triple activity, extended half-life for once weekly dosing, and liver-targeting design.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Development Context</h3>
      <p>The source states that a phase 2b study in biopsy-proven NASH subjects was ongoing in the US and Korea at the time of the presentation.</p>
    </article>
  </div>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211 is a glucagon/GIP/GLP-1 triple agonist with activity features designed to treat NASH and fibrosis.</li>
    <li>In TAA mice, HM15211 improved liver fibrosis, hepatic marker gene expression, and blood liver enzyme readouts.</li>
    <li>In vitro assays showed inhibition of monocyte differentiation, M1 polarization, HSC activation, and collagen secretion.</li>
    <li>The source concludes that HM15211 might provide improved efficacy for treatment of NASH and fibrosis.</li>
