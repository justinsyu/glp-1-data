<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Study design">
    <div>Chronic PD model: MPTP/probenecid intraperitoneal injection twice weekly for 5 weeks, with HM15211 subcutaneously once weekly for 6 weeks.</div>
    <div>AD progression model: 6-week-old db/db mice treated with subcutaneous HM15211 once every 2 days for 12 weeks.</div>
    <div>EAE model: SJL mice received PLP139-151 in complete Freund's adjuvant and pertussis toxin, with HM15211 from day 1.</div>
    <div>PD readouts included motor function, TH+ cells, alpha-synuclein, microglial area, IFN-gamma, and IL-10.</div>
    <div>AD and EAE readouts included A beta 1-42, AGE, cytokines, oxidative stress, demyelination, Treg cells, and IL-10 mRNA.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Mechanistic Frame</h3>
      <p>The poster describes GLP-1, glucagon, and GIP as having neuroprotective effects and evaluates HM15211 as a long-acting triple agonist across multiple brain disease models.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Source Conclusion</h3>
      <p>The source concludes that HM15211's neuroprotective effects were derived from anti-inflammatory properties in neurodegenerative animal models.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>HM15211 inhibited the increase of alpha-synuclein in the MPTP/probenecid chronic PD model and restored motor function.</li>
    <li>HM15211 reversed AD-related pathological characters such as A beta 1-42 and AGE accumulation in aged db/db mice.</li>
    <li>HM15211 reduced EAE clinical score and spinal cord demyelination in the relapsing-remitting EAE model of MS.</li>
    <li>The poster concludes that HM15211 might have therapeutic potential for neurodegenerative diseases.</li>
  </ul>
