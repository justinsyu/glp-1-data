<section>
  <h2>Study At A Glance</h2>
  <div class="flow-diagram" aria-label="Single ascending dose study design">
    <div>Phase 1, randomized, double blind, placebo controlled, single ascending dose study in healthy volunteers.</div>
    <div>Six cohorts of eight participants each, randomized 6 active and 2 placebo.</div>
    <div>Single fasted doses: 1 mg, 10 mg, 15 mg, 30 mg, 60 mg, and 90 mg.</div>
    <div>Cohort 4 assessed food effect with 15 mg fasted on Day 1 and 15 mg fed on Day 4.</div>
    <div>Safety, tolerability, and pharmacokinetics were evaluated; all 36 active participants were included in the PK analysis.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Population</h3>
      <p>Participants were medically healthy males and females aged 18 to 55 years inclusive, with BMI 18.0 to 30.0 kg/m2 and body weight at least 45 kg.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Completion</h3>
      <p>The poster states that all randomized participants completed the study, with 36 receiving GSBR-1290 and 12 receiving pooled placebo.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Physiology Readouts</h2>
  <div class="evidence-grid evidence-grid-2">
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Heart Rate And QTcF</h3>
      <p>The source reports no changes in QTcF and an increasing heart-rate trend over time, peaking at 12 hours after dose and larger in higher dose groups.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="evidence-card">
      <h3>Plasma Glucose</h3>
      <p>No episodes of hypoglycemia were observed. At higher GSBR-1290 dose levels, the poster reports a predominantly decreased mean change from baseline over time in healthy participants.</p>
    </article>

<section>
  <h2>Source Conclusions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>GSBR-1290 was generally safe and well tolerated in this Phase 1 SAD study of healthy participants.</li>
    <li>No serious adverse events and no clinically significant laboratory changes were reported.</li>
    <li>The source reports a dose response in the percent of participants experiencing adverse events.</li>
    <li>The poster states the data supported further clinical development in a multiple ascending dose study in T2D and overweight or obesity.</li>
  </ul>
