# A First-in-human Single Ascending Dose Study of GSBR-1290, a Novel Small Molecule GLP-1 Receptor Agonist, in Healthy Volunteers

Blai Coll,¹ Jinqiang Zhang,² Scott Jacober,¹ Christopher Argent,³ Lani Ibarra,¹ Margo Snyder,¹ Mark Bach¹

¹Structure Therapeutics Inc, South San Francisco, CA, United States; ²Structure Therapeutics, Shanghai, China; ³Scientia Clinical Research

## INTRODUCTION

* Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM) and obesity incidences are rising globally (IDF, 2021). The GLP-1 receptor molecular pathway has been extensively validated, with several glucagon-like peptide 1 receptor agonists (GLP1-RAs) in use for T2DM and chronic weight management (CWM)

* The currently available GLP-1RAs are peptides that mimic the action of GLP-1 at its receptor, however there is a need for GLP-1RAs that can maintain effective concentrations throughout the dosing interval, can be stored easily (no refrigeration) and administered easily (orally with few, if any, restrictions regarding food/fluid intake and concomitant medications)

* GSBR-1290 is a potent small molecule GLP-1RA that can be orally administered. In nonclinical models, GSBR-1290 increased glucose-dependent insulin secretion and suppressed food intake with similar efficacy to the injectable peptide GLP-1RA liraglutide (Mao et al, ADA 2023, Poster 760)

## OBJECTIVE

* The objective of this first in human study was to evaluate the safety and pharmacokinetic (PK) profile of GSBR-1290 through single ascending doses (SAD) in healthy volunteers

## METHODS

* This was a Phase 1, randomized, double blind, placebo controlled, single ascending dose study in healthy volunteers. The safety, tolerability, and PK of GSBR-1290 were evaluated

* In this study, 48 eligible participants were enrolled into 6 dose cohorts of 8 participants each and randomized to receive a single oral dose of GSBR-1290 or placebo (3:1). Single dose escalation proceeded at 1, 10, 15 (fasted/fed), 30, 60 and 90 mg of GSBR 1290 (Figure 1)

* Food effect was investigated in Cohort 4 with a single dose of GSBR-1290 (15 mg) or placebo administered on Day 1 under fasted conditions and another dose administered on Day 4 under fed conditions

* All 36 participants who received GSBR-1290 were included in the PK analysis

* **Key inclusion criteria:**

* Healthy volunteers were included in the study if they satisfied all the following criteria:

    - Males and females aged ≥ 18 years to ≤ 55 years inclusive

    - Body mass index (BMI): ≥ 18.0 to ≤ 30.0 kg/m² with a body weight ≥ 45 kg

    - Medically healthy

* **Key exclusion criteria:**

* History or presence of significant cardiovascular, pulmonary, hepatic, renal, hematological, gastrointestinal (GI), endocrine, immunologic, dermatologic or neurological disease, including any acute illness or surgery within the past 3 months determined by the principal investigator to be clinically significant

* No concomitant medications allowed with the exception of medications used to manage adverse events (AEs) and contraceptives for female participants of childbearing potential

* Cₘₐₓ and AUC increased with dose of GSBR-1290 across the dose range (Figure 2)

* The dose-plasma concentration was found to be non-linear, and may have been influenced by an increase in GI-related side effects

* There was minimal amount of GSBR-1290 excreted unchanged in urine (< 0.1% of the dose, data not shown)

* Reduced systemic exposure of GSBR-1290 when administered in the fed state, with 80% relative bioavailability

* Treatment emergent adverse events (TEAEs) were higher than placebo, with the majority as mild/ moderate (Figure 3)

* Consistent with GLP-1RA class, TEAEs were driven by GI-related side effects

* The most common TEAE was nausea (66% for GSBR-1290 vs 17% in PBO), followed by vomiting (50% for GSBR-1290 vs 1% PBO) and headache (50% vs 25% PBO)

* Other GI-related side effects captured in the study were decreased appetite, diarrhea and abdominal pain

* There was an apparent treatment and dose related trend in the incidence of GI TEAEs

## CHANGE IN HEART RATE

* There were no changes in QTcF (data not shown)

* As expected by the class of GLP1-RA drugs, there was an increasing trend in heart rate over time. The increase peaked at 12h post dose and was larger in high dose groups. Increases in heart rate were observed in the pooled placebo group, but to a much lesser extent

## CHANGE IN PLASMA GLUCOSE

* No episodes of hypoglycemia were observed (data not shown)

* As expected, blood glucose showed an increase from baseline at 6 hours post-dose (ie, 2 hours following lunch) for participants who received GSBR-1290 1 mg and placebo

* At higher GSBR-1290 dose levels, there was predominantly a decrease in the mean change from baseline over time

* These changes in a healthy participant population are not clinically significant and may be due to physiologic variability

## CONCLUSIONS AND DISCUSSION

* GSBR-1290 was generally safe and well-tolerated in this Phase 1 SAD study with healthy participants

* No SAEs and no clinically significant changes in labs

    - Dose-response observed in the percent of participants experiencing AEs, and tolerability profile consistent with GLP-1RA class

* These data support further clinical development in a multiple ascending dose study in individuals with T2DM and participants living with overweight or obesity (currently ongoing)
