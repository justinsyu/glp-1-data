# Significant and Clinically Relevant Weight Changes at 12 Weeks With Small Molecule GLP-1RA, GSBR-1290

Leo Seman, Huibin Yue, Ke (Mike) Liu, Aline Barth, Divya Chari, Michael Pelayo, Blai Coll, Mark Bach

Structure Therapeutics Inc, South San Francisco, CA <span style="float: right;">342</span>

## INTRODUCTION

* Obesity has reached worldwide epidemic proportions, with more than 70% of adults in the United States living with overweight or obesity. Nearly 80% of people with type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) are also living with either overweight or obesity¹

* Selective glucagon-like peptide receptor agonists (GLP-1RAs) are modeled after naturally occurring GLP-1, which functions to reduce appetite and decrease gastric motility

* Several injectable peptide GLP-1RAs are approved for chronic weight management (CWM) but scalability and accessibility remain a challenge globally

* GSBR-1290 is a novel, oral, non-peptide small molecule GLP-1RA in development for CWM and T2DM, as a potential alternative to injectable peptides

* Using structure-based drug discovery, GSBR-1290 was designed to include specific properties, including ~24-hour drug exposure to enable daily (QD) dosing, low risk of off-target safety effects, and large-scale manufacturing capability

## GLP-1RA ORAL SMALL MOLECULE ASPIRATIONAL TARGET PRODUCT PROFILE

* Highly potent, non-peptide, full GLP-1R agonist with minimal β-arrestin signaling engagement

* Weight loss comparable to injectable GLP-1R peptides

* Designed for ~24 hour drug exposure to enable QD dosing in a large patient population

* Clean 6 and 9 month GLP-tox studies with large safety margin (≥ 150 fold at 120 mg)

* Ability to modulate PK properties aimed at optimized tolerability

* Scalability in commercial manufacturing

## METHODS

* These studies were designed to evaluate the safety, tolerability, and efficacy of GSBR-1290 in participants living with overweight or obesity

* Both studies included 12 weeks of exposure to GSBR-1290, with 60 or 120 mg/QD as target doses

- Phase 2a study (GSBR-1290-02)
    - N = 64, randomized 3:1 to GSBR-1290 or placebo
    - Weekly titration scheme, starting at 5 mg up to 120 mg GSBR-1290

- Capsule to tablet PK study (GSBR-1290-05)
    - N = 54 randomized in 3 cohorts
    - Cohorts with differing titration schemes (1, 2, 3 or 4 week) up to 60 mg or 120 mg to assess the impact on tolerability

* The combined data from both studies of percent (%) change of body weight from baseline among the obesity cohorts was analyzed using a mixed effects model for repeated measures. The models incorporate treatment group, visit, treatment-by-visit interaction, and baseline values as covariates. The 120 mg group from the phase 2a and two 120 mg groups (cohort 1 and 2) from the capsule to tablet PK study were combined as single 120 mg group. The placebo groups from both studies were combined as one placebo group

## CONCLUSIONS

* Using structure-based drug discovery, GSBR-1290 was designed to be a potent, orally available, safe, non-peptide small molecule GLP-1RA

* Over 12 weeks across both studies, GSBR-1290 showed clinically meaningful and statistically significant body weight reduction in participants with overweight or obesity

- -6.2 – 6.8% placebo-adjusted weight loss at 60 and 120 mg, respectively, with QD dosing at 12 weeks

- No new safety findings

- Low number of study discontinuations due to AEs

* GSBR-1290 PK findings support QD dosing and proportional exposure

* No permanent elevations in liver enzymes. No DILI and no study discontinuations related to liver function

* A 36-week Phase 2b study of GSBR-1290 in participants living with overweight or obesity is expected to initiate in Q4 2024
