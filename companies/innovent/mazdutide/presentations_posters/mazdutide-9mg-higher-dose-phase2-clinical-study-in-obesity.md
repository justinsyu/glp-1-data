# China Has the Largest Obese and Overweight Population

~500M adults in China are living with obesity or overweight, potentially the largest in the world<sup>i,1</sup>.

* Overweight (BMI 24-27.9kg/m²): 340M

* Obesity (BMI ≥ 28kg/m²): 160M

* Moderate to severe obesity (BMI ≥ 32.5kg/m²): 10M

# Obesity Management is Highly Beneficial in T2DM Treatment

Two digit or more significant weight loss may lead to the remission of T2DM

## ~50% T2DM adult patients
in China are living with overweight or obesity

| Category             | Population (M) |
| -------------------- | -------------- |
| Diabetes             | 140M           |
| Overweight / Obesity | 500M           |


**T2DM incidence rates in Chinese population:**

* 8.8% in BMI< 25 group;
* 13.8% in 25≤BMI<30 group;
* **20.1% in BMI≥30 group.**

Disease Progression and Weight Loss diagram showing icons for person, stomach, blood drop with plus, and hand with glucose monitor. Arrows indicate Disease Progression moving right and Weight Loss moving left.

* <u>**T2DM prevention:**</u> There is strong and consistent evidence that obesity management can delay the progression from prediabetes to T2DM.

* <u>**Beneficial in T2DM treatment:**</u> In patients with T2DM who also have overweight or obesity, modest and sustained weight loss (>5%) has been shown to improve glycemic control, reduce the need for glucose-lowering medications and result in metabolic benefits including lowering of blood pressure and blood lipids, improving insulin resistant and β-cell function.

* <u>**Potential of T2DM remission:**</u> Increased weight loss percentage was associated with increased odds of T2DM remission, starting with 10–15%.
